Extrema:

From school many of you are familiar with this use of the first and second derivative of a function from the discussion of curves:

The vanishing of the first derivative at a point , the criterion for a horizontal tangent in this point, is a necessary  condition for the existence of a local extremum. The condition cannot be sufficient because a horizontal turning tangent may occur.

Sufficient  conditions for a local maximum or minimum can be obtained only by looking at the second derivative: means a turn to the left, i.e. a local minimum, while indicates a turn to the right, thus a maximum.
