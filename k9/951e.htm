9 Vectors

9.5 Scalar Product and the Kronecker Symbol

9.5.1 Motivation

In order to describe the orthogonality of our basis vectors, and also for physical reasons, we need a product of two vectors which measures the angle between the two factors and is connected with the projection of one vector onto the other. For constrained motions (for instance along rails or on an inclined plane) not the entire applied force, but only its projection (onto the rails or onto the plane) is the crucial physical quantity. Also for the computation of the work which must be done in order to displace a mass against a force (e.g. the force of gravity) not the entire force, but only its projection onto the displacement direction is the really determining quantity.
