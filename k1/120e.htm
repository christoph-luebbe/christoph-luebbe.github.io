1.2 Physical Quantities

In the development of  physics it turned out again and again how difficult, but also important it was to develop the most suitable concepts and find the relevant quantities (e.g. force or energy) in terms of which nature can be described both simply and comprehensively.

Every physical quantity  consists of a  measured value and a  measuring unit, i.e. a pure number and a dimension. All difficulties in conversations are avoided, if we treat both parts like a product "value times dimension".
