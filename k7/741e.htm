7.4.1 Indefinite Integral

With this general knowledge about the integral concept we want to turn to the all-deciding question of the calculation of  Riemann integrals, in which the Fundamental Theorem of Differential and Integral Calculus helps us decisively, as its name promises.

At first we want to expand the Riemann integral concept: the "definite" or Riemann integral had assigned to a  bounded continuous function , called the integrand, by a given lower interval borderand upper border, the (signed) area under the function, meaning a real number. Mathematicians call such an entity a functional. Now we are interested in how this area will change, when we move the upper border.
